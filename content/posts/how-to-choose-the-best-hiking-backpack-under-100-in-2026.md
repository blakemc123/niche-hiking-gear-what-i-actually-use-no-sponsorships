#### Capacity and Size
When choosing a backpack, the first thing to consider is the capacity and size. The size of the backpack should match the length of your hike. For day hikes, a 20-30 liter backpack is usually sufficient. For multi-day hikes, you might need a larger capacity, but under $100, you'll likely find backpacks in the 20-40 liter range. 

#### Comfort and Fit
Comfort is key. A backpack that fits well and distributes weight evenly can make a huge difference in your hiking experience. Look for backpacks with padded straps and a padded back panel. Adjustability of the straps is also important, as it allows you to customize the fit to your body.

### Materials and Durability

#### Water Resistance
Water resistance is crucial, especially if you're hiking in areas with unpredictable weather. Look for backpacks made with materials that can withstand moisture, such as nylon or polyester. Some backpacks come with a rain cover, which can be a lifesaver.

#### Weight and Durability
A backpack should be lightweight but also durable. Lightweight materials can make your hike more comfortable, but they should not compromise on durability. Check reviews and materials to ensure the backpack can handle the wear and tear of hiking.

## Practical Advice for Choosing a Backpack

### How to Measure Your Back and Choose the Right Size
- Measure the distance from the base of your neck to the top of your hip bone. This will give you an idea of the backpack's length.
- Consider the width of your shoulders to ensure the backpack's straps will fit comfortably.

### Tips for Adjusting Straps and Finding Comfort
- Adjust the shoulder straps so that the backpack sits snugly on your back.
- Tighten the hip belt to transfer weight from your shoulders to your hips.
- Adjust the sternum strap to keep the backpack in place and prevent it from moving around.

### How to Test a Backpack Before Buying
- Try on the backpack with a full load to see how it feels.
- Walk around with it to see if it's comfortable and if the straps fit well.
- Check the durability of the material and the quality of the stitching.
